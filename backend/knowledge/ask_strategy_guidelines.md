# Ask Strategy Guidelines

Internal guidance for setting the recommended ask amount in donor appeals. These principles inform, but never override, the deterministic ask-ladder computed from a donor's giving history.

## Anchor on prior giving

The single best predictor of the next gift is the last gift. A well-tested ask ladder presents the donor's typical gift, a modest step up (roughly 1.5x), and an aspirational amount (roughly 2.5x). Never lead with the aspirational figure alone.

## Match the ask to the segment

Lapsing donors respond better to a gentle, reconnecting ask near their prior level, paired with a reminder of impact. Loyal recurring donors can be invited to increase their commitment. High-value and major donors warrant a personal, relationship-based approach and asks that should be reviewed by a gift officer before sending.

## Ground the ask in impact

Every ask is more effective when tied to a concrete outcome the donor can picture — a month of care, a surgery, a spay/neuter procedure. Use the published cost-of-care figures rather than abstract appeals.

## When to escalate for review

Any recommended ask in the major-gift range, any ask to a donor flagged for stewardship sensitivity, and any case where the giving history is too thin to anchor confidently should be routed to a human reviewer rather than mailed automatically.

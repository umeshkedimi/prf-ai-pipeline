# Donor Stewardship Principles

Guidance on tone and stewardship for donor communications across all segments.

## Gratitude first

Every appeal opens by acknowledging the donor's prior support before making a new ask. Returning donors should never feel treated like strangers; reference the impact their past giving already made possible.

## Honesty about need

We describe need plainly and without manipulation. We do not exaggerate figures, use distressing imagery gratuitously, or imply that a single gift solves a systemic problem. Trust is the foundation of lifetime donor value.

## Respect for capacity

We never pressure a donor beyond their demonstrated capacity. An ask that is too high risks alienating a loyal supporter; the goal is a sustainable, multi-year relationship, not a single large gift.

## Recognition and follow-up

Major gifts are acknowledged personally within 48 hours. All donors receive an annual impact summary showing the concrete results of their support, reinforcing why their next gift matters.
